# Colors

---

For accessibility and consistency reasons, we use a limited color palette.

The
colors are divided into four categories:

- **Neutral colors** are used for text, borders, and backgrounds.
- **Brand colors** are used for the main elements of the interface, such as
  buttons, links, and icons;
- **Decorative colors** can only be used in illustration and low hierarchy
  backgrounds;
- **Feedback colors** are used to highlight feedbacks for the user;

## Neutral colors

Those colors are the basic colors of the interface.

They are mostly used for texts, strokes, borders and some backgrounds.

## Decorative colors

Those lightly shaded colors are mainly used for illustration and low hierarchy
backgrounds. They can also be used on hovers and active states.

However, you should never use those colors as text and/or icons colors, strokes,
border and/or separator and background for interactive elements _(e.g. buttons)_.

> ##### ⚠️ **Attention**
>
> ---
>
> Decorative Colors have low contrast color on light background, meaning they
> have a low accessibility performance for some users with visibility issues.  
> So these colors can be used for decoration **ONLY**, never use it for actions,
> interaction neither text colors. Only for static backgrounds with low
> hierarchy!

## Feedback colors

Those colors are meant to be used to give feedback to the user after an action
or an interaction _(e.g. as borders, toast background, text color, ...)_.

In particular, `.50` tones are used as text/icon and border color when giving a
feedback.

> ##### ⚠️ **Attention**
>
> ---
>
> You should use these colors only in the context of a feedback.
